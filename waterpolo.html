Water polo is a contact, team sport, consisting of two teams of 7 players. The players all play in a pool so that they cannot touch the ground with their lil' piggies with the risk of possible drowning. The teams score points by throwing the ball into their opponents goal.

There are a few positions in the pool

  • Goalkeeper: This player tries to block the oppoens scoring attemps on their goal by using their arms and torso to block the ball
  • Field players: These players play both offense and efense as they try to prevent the opposing team from scoring and try to score on their opponents goal
  • Rubber ducky manager: This player is arguably the most important in the pool, they keep track of and manage all of the rubber ducks, bath crayons, and other bath toys.

Penalties and plays and information

  • Any player who controls the ball using two hands is given a penalty, with the exception of the goalie, provided they are within 5 meters of the goal
  • A player can hold, pass, block, or catch the ball with one hand only, this is called controlling the ball.
  • Goalkeepers have special allowances within 5 meters of their goal such as punching the ball and controlling it using two hands.
  • A pass is considered a wet pass when the pall hits the water before the reciving player and catch the ball.
  • Because water polo takes place in a pool, placers have to wear swimsuits, goggles, ear protectors, and swim caps.
